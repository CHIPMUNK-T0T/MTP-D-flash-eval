# Strategic Guide for Local Governments: Implementing Generative AI

## 1. Clarifying the Purpose of Adoption
**Objective:** To move beyond hype and define a clear, measurable "Why" that aligns AI with local government missions.

**Concrete Example:**
Instead of adopting AI simply because "it's cool," a city like **Oakwood, Ohio** adopts AI to specifically address the "Great Recession" of 2020. Their purpose is to automate the manual processing of thousands of housing applications and eviction notices. By using AI to triage applications based on income and credit history, Oakwood reduces processing time from 48 hours to 15 minutes, allowing staff to focus on complex legal reviews rather than data entry.

## 2. Drafting Internal Guidelines
**Objective:** To establish a governance framework that ensures ethical use, transparency, and accountability.

**Concrete Example:**
Oakwood creates the **"AI Governance Charter,"** which mandates that all AI models used for public services must undergo a "Human-in-the-Loop" review before deployment. The charter explicitly prohibits the use of AI for decisions affecting individual rights (e.g., bail decisions or parole) without human oversight. It also requires a "Data Privacy Impact Assessment" (DPIA) for every dataset used, ensuring that sensitive citizen information is never exposed to untrained models.

## 3. Security and Personal Data Protection
**Objective:** To mitigate the risk of data breaches and ensure compliance with regulations like GDPR or CCPA.

**Concrete Example:**
Following a minor incident where a prototype model accidentally leaked a list of names, Oakwood implements a **"Zero Trust" architecture** for all AI systems. This means no device, regardless of whether it is internal or external, is trusted by default. They also deploy **federated learning**, where the AI model trains on local data without ever sending raw citizen records to a central cloud server, ensuring that even if the server is compromised, the data remains secure.

## 4. Budgeting and Procurement
**Objective:** To manage the high costs of AI infrastructure while ensuring fiscal responsibility.

**Concrete Example:**
Oakwood avoids purchasing expensive proprietary models by adopting a **"Public-Private Partnership" (PPP) model**. They partner with a local tech firm to build a custom, open-source model specifically for their needs.
